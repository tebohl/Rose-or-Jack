Data Modeling

Using a complete Titanic passenger dataset, our team focused on the following, pertinent features: sex, age, passenger class, survival outcome, and the total number of relatives onboard. The data was cleaned, hot coded, split into train and test data, and scaled. Specific models tested as follows:

Logistic Regression

Using Scikit-learn's Logistic Regression at 1,000 iterations, an 83% test score was achieved.

K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN)

As shown in this chart, eleven tested as the best possible cluster value using KNN, with a higher test score of approximately 85%.

Deep Neural Network (DNN)

After multiple tests, our team's most optimized DNN model ran at 1,621 parameters, with three hidden layers--relu, tanh, relu--and sigmoid as output layer, 100 epochs. Despite the permutations made, 80% remained as the highest test score achieved.

Decision Tree

Scikit-learn's Random Forest Classifier provided the most successful model with the strongest 85% margin. A deeper look into the features showed that age had the most significant impact on survival rate, seconded by passenger class.
